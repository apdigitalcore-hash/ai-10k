SECTION 2: YOUR FIRST 3 PROMPTS TO RUN
------------------------------------------------------------
These three prompts will immediately demonstrate what Claude can
do for your business. Run all three before doing anything else.

--- FIRST PROMPT: MARKETING ---
Purpose: Create a week of social content in under 10 minutes.

Paste this into Claude (fill in your details):

[specific result]. My tone is [professional/casual/bold/warm].

Write a follow-up email that:
- Opens with a genuine, non-desperate tone
- References something specific from our conversation

SECTION 3: HOW TO CUSTOMIZE PROMPTS FOR YOUR BUSINESS
------------------------------------------------------------
Every prompt in the playbook uses placeholder brackets like
[your niche], [ideal client], and [desired outcome]. Here's
how to fill them in so outputs are specific, not generic.

THE 5 VARIABLES TO ALWAYS INCLUDE:

1. YOUR ROLE: What you do and how you describe yourself.
   Bad: "I'm a coach"
   Good: "I'm a mindset and business coach for women leaving
   corporate jobs to start their own consulting businesses"

2. YOUR IDEAL CLIENT: The more specific, the better the output.
   Bad: "small business owners"
   Good: "female service business owners in their 30s-40s who
   are making $3K-$7K/month and want to reach $15K/month
   without working more hours"

3. THEIR PAIN: The real problem they want solved.
   Bad: "they're struggling with marketing"
   Good: "they post consistently but don't generate leads.
   They're getting engagement but no one's buying."

4. YOUR OFFER: What you sell and what it costs.
   Be specific: name, format, price, duration, deliverables.

5. TONE AND VOICE: How you want to sound.
   Options: professional, warm, direct, conversational,
   bold, educational, motivational, no-nonsense.

PRO TIP — Build a Context Block:
Write a 5-10 sentence paragraph describing your business once.
Save it as a note. Paste it at the top of every Claude prompt.
You'll never have to re-explain your business again.

Example Context Block:
"[Your name] is a [role] who helps [ideal client] achieve
[result] through [your method/offer]. Their tone is [tone].
Their ideal client is [description]. Their main offer is
[offer name], priced at [$X], delivered [format]. Their
biggest differentiator is [what makes you different]."

SECTION 4: PROMPT STACKING TECHNIQUE
------------------------------------------------------------
Prompt stacking is the practice of feeding the output of one
Claude prompt directly into the next — compounding the value
of each response and building toward a complete deliverable.

HOW IT WORKS:
Step 1: Run Prompt A. Get the output.
Step 2: Copy the output.
Step 3: Start a new prompt with: "Using the content below as
your source material, [new instruction]..." then paste the output.

REAL EXAMPLE — BUILDING A SALES EMAIL FROM A SOCIAL POST:

Prompt 1 (Content): "Write a LinkedIn post about [topic] from
the perspective of a [your role]. Make it story-based, 200 words,
and end with a hook to learn more."

(Claude writes the post. Copy it.)

Prompt 2 (Sales): "Using the post below as your source material,
expand it into a full sales email. Keep the story intact but add:
- A more specific pain point in paragraph 2
- Social proof (you can use placeholder client results)
- A clear offer description
- A CTA to [your call-to-action]
Keep it under 400 words. [Paste post here]"

(Claude writes the email. Copy it.)

Prompt 3 (Ads): "Using the email below, extract the single most
compelling line or idea and turn it into 3 Facebook or Instagram
ad hooks (under 3 sentences each). [Paste email here]"

Three outputs. One core idea. 15 minutes of work.

MORE STACKING IDEAS:
- Blog post → Email newsletter → Social post series
- Discovery call notes → Proposal → Follow-up email
- Client testimonial → Case study → Social proof ad
- Podcast transcript → Blog post → LinkedIn article → Tweet thread
- One offer → Full sales page → Email sequence → DM script

SECTION 5: COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID
------------------------------------------------------------
MISTAKE 1: Being too vague in your prompts.
Fix: Add context, specificity, and examples. Claude produces
better output when it knows who you are and who you're talking to.

MISTAKE 2: Accepting the first draft as final.
Fix: Always ask Claude to "revise this to be more [direct/personal/
punchy/specific]. Here's what's missing: [describe the gap]."
The second and third drafts are almost always significantly better.

MISTAKE 3: Using Claude without a persona.
Fix: Start every session with your Context Block (see Section 3).
Treat Claude like a new team member who needs a brief — because it does.

MISTAKE 4: Generating content that sounds like AI.
Telltale signs: "Delve into", "In today's fast-paced world",
"It's important to note", "Leverage", "Game-changer."
Fix: Add to every prompt: "Write in plain, conversational language.
Avoid corporate buzzwords and AI-sounding filler phrases. Sound
like a real person talking to a peer."

MISTAKE 5: Running one prompt and stopping.
Fix: Use the prompt stacking technique. One good output becomes
10 great assets with 3 follow-up prompts.

MISTAKE 6: Not saving good outputs.
Fix: Keep a running Google Doc of Claude outputs that worked.
This becomes your personal content swipe file over time.

MISTAKE 7: Asking Claude to do too many things in one prompt.
Fix: Break complex tasks into smaller prompts. "Write my entire
marketing strategy" produces generic output. "Write the positioning
statement for my [offer] targeting [audience]" produces a gem.

SECTION 6: PRO TIPS FOR BETTER CLAUDE OUTPUTS
------------------------------------------------------------
TIP 1 — GIVE IT A ROLE
Open every prompt with: "You are an expert [copywriter/
strategist/sales coach/etc.] who specializes in [niche]."
This primes Claude to respond with the right expertise level.

TIP 2 — SPECIFY THE FORMAT
Tell Claude exactly how you want the output structured:
"Write this as a numbered list." / "Use short paragraphs under
3 sentences." / "Format with headers for each section." /
"Output this as a table with columns for [X, Y, Z]."

TIP 3 — SET THE WORD COUNT
"Keep this under 200 words" or "Write at least 500 words" gives
Claude boundaries that improve output quality dramatically.

TIP 4 — USE REAL EXAMPLES
"Here's an example of content I've written that I liked: [paste
example]. Match this tone and style." This is the fastest way to
get outputs that actually sound like you.

TIP 5 — ASK FOR OPTIONS
"Give me 5 different versions of this headline, ranging from
direct to emotional to curiosity-based." Then pick the best one
or combine elements from multiple versions.

TIP 6 — CRITIQUE AND REVISE IN THE SAME CHAT
You don't need to start a new conversation every time. After an
output, say: "This is good but [specific issue]. Rewrite section
2 to be more direct and remove the last paragraph entirely."
Claude holds context within the conversation.

TIP 7 — ASK CLAUDE TO THINK LIKE YOUR CLIENT
"Before you write this, describe what my ideal client is thinking
and feeling when they encounter this content. Then write it for
that emotional state." The output becomes dramatically more targeted.

TIP 8 — ADD NEGATIVE CONSTRAINTS
Tell Claude what NOT to do: "Do not use motivational language. Do
not end with a question. Do not mention AI tools. Do not use bullet
points — write in full paragraphs only."
Negative constraints often produce the most distinctive outputs.

DAY 3 — CONTENT ENGINE (30 minutes)

DAY 4 — OPERATIONS CLEANUP (45 minutes)

Options: write a client onboarding SOP, document your service

draft a standard scope-of-work template.
Output: A system that saves you time every single week going forward.

DAY 5 — LEAD GENERATION ASSET (60 minutes)
Task: Pick one lead magnet from your "20 Lead Magnet Blueprints"

[name and description]. Make it specific, practical, and

DAY 6 — OFFER SHARPENING (30 minutes)
Task: Use Prompts 41-50 to sharpen your core offer or create
a new one. Focus on: positioning, pricing, or the value stack.
Run this prompt: "Review my current offer: [describe it].
Identify 3 ways I could reposition it to feel more premium,
more specific, or more urgent. Then rewrite the offer

Output: A stronger offer that commands higher prices.

DAY 7 — REVIEW AND STACK (30 minutes)
Task: Collect the best outputs from Days 1-6.
Use prompt stacking to connect them:
- Turn your Day 3 content into a nurture email sequence
- Turn your Day 5 lead magnet into an opt-in page headline
- Turn your Day 6 offer rewrite into a social proof post
Output: A cohesive system where your content, offers, and
lead generation all reinforce each other.
